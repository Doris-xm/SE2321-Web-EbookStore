The Great Gatsby, written by F. Scott Fitzgerald, epitomizes the Jazz Age of the 1920s in America. Set in the fictional town of West Egg on Long Island, the novel explores themes of wealth, idealism, and the American Dream. Through its compelling characters, it portrays the excesses and moral emptiness of the era, showcasing lavish parties, societal disintegration, and the pursuit of unattainable dreams.
